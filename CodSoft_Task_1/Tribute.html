Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein was a German-born physicist who developed the special and general theories of relativity. He won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect. His work is also known for its influence on the philosophy of science.





Timeline


  • June 1905

    He published four groundbreaking papers on the photoelectric effect, Brownian motion, special relativity, and mass-energy equivalence.

  • January 1906

    Receives his doctorate from the University of Zurich and becomes a privatdozent at Bern University

  • November 1915

    He completed the general theory of relativity, which describes gravity as a consequence of the curvature of space-time.

  • June 1916

    He predicted the existence of gravitational waves, which were detected in 2016.

  • March 1917

    He introduced the concept of stimulated emission, which laid the foundation for the invention of the laser.

  • December 1922

    He received the Nobel Prize for his explanation of the photoelectric effect, which showed that light has both wave and particle properties.

  • August 1939

    In 1939, he wrote a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt, warning him of the possibility of nuclear weapons and urging him to support atomic research.

  • March 1950

    He published his last scientific paper, which attempted to unify the forces of nature in a single theory.
